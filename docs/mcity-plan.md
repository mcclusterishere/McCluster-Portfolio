# M City — the open-world product plan

The pitch in one line: **GTA where the missions are real.** The map
is the platform; the districts are its wings; every mission is a
verifiable real-life move (a play, a claim, a deal, a poll answer)
and the rewards are real (M Tokens, ranks, badges, unlocked rooms).
No studio can clone it, because the game engine is a marketplace.

## v0.1 — SHIPPED (mcity.html)

Top-down drivable city in the browser (canvas, 60fps, keyboard +
tap-to-drive). Six districts mapped to the real platform:

| District | Wing | Unlock condition (real) |
|---|---|---|
| The Studio | the music house | open from the jump |
| The Back Lots | the claim run | 1 real play |
| The Exchange | the market | 3 real plays |
| The Bureau | SCB | 10 civic points on the PSMF ride |
| City Hall | Equity Uprise | 30 civic points |
| The Vault | the M Chain | Keyholder on the claim run |

Missions inside each district deep-link to the real feature and
complete off the same stores the engines write (plays, claim moves,
civic CP, grind streaks). REP = a composite of real activity.
Nothing in the game is game-only make-believe.

## The staged road to "similar to GTA"

- **v0.2 — the living city:** day/night synced to the app's clock,
  NPC cars, the radio (the actual music player riding the whip),
  mission markers with GPS-style routing, district interiors as
  styled rooms instead of link-outs.
- **v0.3 — identity in-world:** your ticker on your whip, your page
  vibe colors your character, other real members' HQs appear on the
  map (their page = their building), leaderboards by REP.
- **v0.4 — the economy in-world:** M Token balance spendable on
  cosmetic unlocks (whips, building skins) — closed loop preserved;
  deals initiated from inside the world (drive to someone's HQ →
  propose).
- **v1.0 — the 3D city:** Three.js low-poly night city (the aurora
  palette), same mission engine underneath. Browser-first keeps it
  in the PWA; no app-store gate.
- **The honest line:** a true GTA-class product (streaming open
  world, physics, story missions) is a studio-scale, multi-year,
  multi-million build. The moat is NOT the graphics — it's the
  real-mission verification engine, which is live today. Graphics
  scale with revenue; the mechanic never has to change.

## The design laws
1. Every mission is verifiable by the platform's own witnessed data.
2. Rewards are the platform's real instruments (tokens, ranks,
   badges) — never a parallel fake economy.
3. The game reads the same stores the engines write; one truth.
4. Closed-loop rules apply in-world: nothing bought with tokens can
   cash out.
