# The Press Engine — how "Matthew McCluster" becomes notable

On-site SEO gets you ranked for your own name. **Independent press coverage** is
what gets you a Knowledge Panel, authority backlinks, and — eventually — the
Wikipedia notability bar. This is the one thing a website cannot manufacture, so
here it is done for you: a release you can send tonight, the pitch email, and who
to send it to. Everything below is factual — send it as-is or tighten it.

---

## 3. Who to send it to (build the coverage that counts)

**Connecticut (Bridgeport angle):**
- CT Post (Connecticut Post) — Bridgeport desk
- Hearst CT Media locals
- News 12 Connecticut
- WICZ / WTNH / FOX61 community desks
- Bridgeport-specific: OIB (Only In Bridgeport) blog, Bridgeport Times

**Georgia (Acworth / civic-leadership angle):**
- Cobb County Courier · Marietta Daily Journal (Acworth is in Cobb County)
- Atlanta Journal-Constitution community desk
- 11Alive / WSB-TV community tips

**Beat / national (civic-tech, founders):**
- Qwoted and Connectively (formerly HARO) — answer reporter queries daily; every
  answer that gets used is a backlink from a real outlet. This is the fastest
  route to independent citations.
- Local business journals (Hartford Business Journal, Atlanta Business Chronicle)
- Podcast circuit: civic / founder / music-tech shows

**Why this matters for Wikipedia:** Wikipedia needs *multiple independent,
reliable secondary sources* with significant coverage. Two or three real articles
from the outlets above — not press-release reprints, actual reported pieces — is
the threshold. Once they exist, an independent editor can create the article (or
submit via Articles for Creation). Government proclamations are primary sources:
they help, but they don't count on their own.

## 4. Working it (the cadence)

1. Send the pitch to 5–10 local reporters this week. Follow up once, politely, after 4 days.
2. Answer 2–3 Qwoted/Connectively queries a day in your lane (civic tech, small
   business, music, Connecticut/Georgia). Each placement is a backlink.
3. Every time you get coverage, add it to `newsroom.html` (link out to the article)
   and it compounds your entity authority.
4. Keep the boilerplate identical everywhere — same name, same site, same one-line
   description. Consistency is what Google merges into one entity.
