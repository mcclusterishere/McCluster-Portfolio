Why paid reach, and why it's imperative

Community data does not collect itself. The residents whose voices matter most are not filling out government forms. They are on Instagram and Facebook. A focused ad budget of $500 to $1,000, targeted to specific ZIP codes, puts this intake line directly in front of a district and turns scrolling into signal.

Reach where people already areMeta's ZIP-level targeting puts the form in front of residents block by block, not the whole internet. The right neighborhoods, on the apps they already open all day.
Turn spend into structured dataEvery dollar becomes measurable reach and real responses. Who, where, and what they need, captured cleanly instead of guessed at.
Evidence, not anecdoteThe result is a dataset an office and its partners can act on and cite. Grant applications and policy asks land harder when they are backed by real resident input.
A fraction of the alternativeCanvassing a district costs thousands in staff hours. A targeted campaign gathers the same signal for a fraction of that, in days instead of months.
