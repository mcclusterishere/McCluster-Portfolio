The record on you

Privacy.

Plain words, no fog: the house keeps what it needs to run your account and your deals, processes money through companies built for money, and hands your data to nobody else. Updated July 2026.

What the house keeps

  • Your account — an ID, and your email if you signed in with one. Instant accounts carry no email at all.
  • Your listing — the name, ticker, headline, photo, links and terms you chose to publish. That part is public on purpose: it IS your page.
  • Your deals and messages — the record both sides see: amounts, signatures, notes, status.
  • Your activity — which pages and features get used (plays, taps, deals), tied to your account ID when signed in, kept to make the product and your score real.

Who touches money

Stripe runs card checkout, payouts and identity checks; Square runs the optional donation links. Card numbers, bank accounts and ID documents live with them, on their pages, under their vaults and their compliance — none of it ever touches this site's database. The house stores only the verdicts: paid, verified, payouts-enabled.

Who else sees signals

Google Analytics counts visits and taps so the desk can see what works. The database (accounts, listings, deals) runs on Supabase. That's the whole list — no data brokers, no ad-sale, no selling you.

On your device

The site keeps your session and preferences in your browser's local storage so you stay signed in and the app remembers your card. No third-party tracking cookies.

Your moves

  • See it — your desk and your public page show everything the house holds on you.
  • Fix it — edit your listing any time; the mirror presses changes in live.
  • Delete it — write matthew@mccluster.org from your account email and the record comes down. Deal records both sides signed stay, as ledgers do.
