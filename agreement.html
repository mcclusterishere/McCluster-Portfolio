Equity Uprise · A Private Member Association

The Member
Agreement.

Equity Uprise is a private member association, not a public storefront. Opening an account is joining — becoming a Chaser — and joining is signing this. It's written to be read: what membership means, how the credit and the equity pool work, how deals run, and how we settle differences like members instead of strangers.

Version 2 · July 2026 · signed at account opening

1 · The Association

Equity Uprise is a private member association operating under McCluster Corp, a Connecticut nonprofit — a private membership program of that organization, not a separate business and not a public marketplace. Membership is voluntary, free, and revocable, and it opens when you create an account and accept this Agreement. Members are called Chasers. Chasers deal with one another inside the Association's platform — listings, deals, bookings, credit, and records are member-to-member activity within a private association, and the Association sets the rules of its own house: who may list, what stays on the floor, and how the record is kept.

2 · E-Up credit

  • 1 E⤴︎ = $1 of platform credit, always. It is a member benefit — not money, not a deposit, not a security, and it never floats.
  • Two colors, one law: credit earned by work (completed deals, bounties, services rendered) may be redeemed — that is the Association paying a Chaser for work, reviewed and approved by the desk. Credit purchased or received as a gift or grant spends inside the platform and is not redeemable for cash.
  • Purchases fund the Equity Reserve; the books are published at reserve.html.
  • Credit carries no interest, no expiration, and no fees to hold. The Association may retire promotional grants (like beta bankrolls) with notice on the reserve page.

3 · The equity pool

Every real transaction on the platform draws one percent into the equity pool, and the Chaser whose transaction it was accrues a matching stake. Read this carefully, because the name can mislead:

  • The equity pool is a membership participation benefit — a way the Association recognizes the Chasers who build the house. A stake tracks how much you've moved through the platform, nothing more.
  • It is not ownership of McCluster Corp or the Association, not a share, not a security, not a promise of profit, dividend, or cash payout, and it does not float in value. It is non-cashable by design.
  • Any future benefit tied to the pool is discretionary to the Association and, where relevant, would be introduced only under counsel. Nothing here grants a legal or financial interest of any kind.

4 · Deals between Chasers

Deals, bookings, and splits are agreements between the Chasers who sign them. The Association provides the venue, the record, and the rails — it is not a party to your deal, doesn't guarantee anyone's work, and the platform fee is baked into the one price the paying side sees. The signed record on both desks is the agreement of record.

5 · Your record and your data

The platform keeps first-party records of member activity — plays, deals, payments, taps — to run the floor, compute standings like the Street Score, and improve the house. Your private numbers stay yours (worth, score, wallet, equity stake); public standings show points, never dollars. The Association doesn't sell member data. Supporters' contact lists belong to the Chaser who earned them.

6 · Conduct

The house rules: real names on signatures, no fraud, no impersonating another Chaser's ticker or brand, no using the rails to move money for anything unlawful, no harassment on the floor. The Association may pause or close a membership that breaks the rules; earned credit standing at closure is honored per Section 2.

7 · Disputes — arbitration, member to member

We settle differences inside the Association first: a written complaint to the desk, thirty days to resolve it. What can't be resolved goes to binding individual arbitration under the rules of a recognized arbitration provider — not to court, and not as a class action. Both sides waive class and collective proceedings. Small-claims court for qualifying amounts stays available to either side.

8 · The honest limits

The Association is not your lawyer, accountant, or bank. Being a private member association does not exempt anyone — including the Association — from laws that apply to its activity, including securities, money-transmission, consumer-protection, and tax law. Services are provided as-is; the Association's total liability to a Chaser is capped at the greater of $100 or the fees that Chaser paid the house in the prior twelve months.

9 · Changes

The Agreement can be amended; the version number and date up top change when it does, Chasers get notice on their desk, and continuing to use the platform after notice is acceptance. Your signature (version, time, and account) lives in the Association's records — and on your desk.
