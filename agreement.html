The M Network Association

The Member
Agreement.

This platform is a private association, not a public storefront. Opening an account is joining — and joining is signing this. It's written to be read: what membership means, how the credit works, how deals run, and how we settle differences like members instead of strangers.

Version 1 · July 2026 · signed at account opening

1 · The Association

The M Network Association operates under the Equity Uprise nonprofit. Membership is voluntary, free, and opens when you create an account and accept this Agreement. Members deal with each other inside the Association's floor — listings, deals, bookings, credit, and records are member-to-member activity within a private platform, and the Association sets the rules of its own house: who may list, what stays on the floor, and how the record is kept.

2 · E-Up credit

  • 1 E⤴︎ = $1 of platform credit, always. It is a member benefit — not money, not a deposit, not a security, and it never floats.
  • Two colors, one law: credit earned by work (completed deals, bounties, services rendered) may be redeemed — that is the Association paying a member for work. Credit purchased or received as a gift or grant spends inside the platform and is not redeemable for cash.
  • Purchases fund the Equity Reserve; the books are published at reserve.html.
  • Credit carries no interest, no expiration, and no fees to hold. The Association may retire promotional grants (like beta bankrolls) with notice on the reserve page.

3 · Deals between members

Deals, bookings, and splits are agreements between the members who sign them. The Association provides the venue, the record, and the rails — it is not a party to your deal, doesn't guarantee anyone's work, and the platform fee is baked into the one price the paying side sees. The signed record on both desks is the agreement of record.

4 · Your record and your data

The platform keeps first-party records of member activity — plays, deals, payments, taps — to run the floor, compute standings like the Street Score, and improve the house. Your private numbers stay yours (worth, score, wallet); public standings show points, never dollars. The Association doesn't sell member data. Supporters' contact lists belong to the member who earned them.

5 · Conduct

The house rules: real names on signatures, no fraud, no impersonating another member's ticker, no using the rails to move money for anything unlawful, no harassment on the floor. The Association may pause or close a membership that breaks the rules; earned credit standing at closure is honored per Section 2.

6 · Disputes — arbitration, member to member

We settle differences inside the Association first: a written complaint to the desk, thirty days to resolve it. What can't be resolved goes to binding individual arbitration in Georgia under the rules of a recognized arbitration provider — not to court, and not as a class action. Both sides waive class and collective proceedings. Small-claims court for qualifying amounts stays available to either side.

7 · The honest limits

The Association is not your lawyer, accountant, or bank. Membership doesn't exempt anyone — including the Association — from laws that apply to commercial activity. Services are provided as-is; the Association's total liability to a member is capped at the greater of $100 or the fees that member paid the house in the prior twelve months. Georgia law governs.

8 · Changes

The Agreement can be amended; the version number and date up top change when it does, members get notice on their desk, and continuing to use the floor after notice is acceptance. Your signature (version, time, and account) lives in the Association's records — and on your desk.
